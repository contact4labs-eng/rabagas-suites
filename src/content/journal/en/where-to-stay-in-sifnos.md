There are five sensible places to base yourself on Sifnos. The choice changes the shape of your week more than people usually expect.

## Apollonia — central, social, walkable

The capital village, in the middle of the island. The pedestrian alley runs through it, end to end. Most of the better restaurants are here. The KTEL buses to every beach leave from here. You can walk to dinner. You sleep where the light is gentlest because the village is on a hill, away from the harbour glare.

**Best for:** first-time visitors, couples, foodies, travellers who don't want a car, repeat visitors who already love it.
**Trade-off:** none, really, if you choose your block carefully — some places sit on the bar lane and stay loud. Streets one alley over are silent by midnight.

## Artemónas — quieter than Apollonia, ten minutes' walk away

Twenty minutes uphill from Apollonia, connected by the marble pedestrian alley. Slightly more genteel — bigger mansions, more bougainvillea, fewer bars. You still have the bus connections via Apollonia, you still have several great restaurants, you just have a calmer evening.

**Best for:** couples who want privacy without isolation, returning visitors, slow-travel weeks.
**Trade-off:** slightly less in the way of going-out energy.

## Kamares — beach + harbour

The port. There's a long sandy beach and a row of beach tavernas. Easy ferry day, easy beach mornings. The downside is everything else takes a drive or a bus.

**Best for:** very short stays (one or two nights), or travellers whose main interest is the beach itself.
**Trade-off:** every village dinner is a logistics decision.

## Vathí or Platis Gialós — beach-locked

These are the two main south-coast beach hamlets. You wake up on the sand. You also need a car for almost everything else.

**Best for:** families with very young children, beach-first travellers.
**Trade-off:** the rhythm of the island happens elsewhere.

## Kastro — the medieval cliff village

A handful of properties exist inside Kastro. It is breathtaking. It is also remote: there is one taverna inside the village, no parking, and a 25-minute drive back to Apollonia in the dark.

**Best for:** writers, photographers, second-trip travellers who already know the island.
**Trade-off:** logistics get serious.

## The honest summary

For most people, Apollonia is the right answer. It puts you within a five-minute walk of dinner, within fifteen minutes' drive of every beach, and within ten minutes' walk of every other village. It is the village that holds the rhythm of the island.
