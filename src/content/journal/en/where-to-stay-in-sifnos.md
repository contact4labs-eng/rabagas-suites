There are five sensible places to base yourself on Sifnos. The choice changes the shape of your week more than people usually expect.

## Short answer

- **Best overall**: Apollonia — the heart of the island, walkable, lively in the evenings, the right call for couples, foodies, and travellers without a car.
- **Best for beach mornings**: Platis Gialós — long sandy beach, a row of tavernas at the door.
- **Most romantic**: A boutique suite in central Apollonia plus an evening in Kastro at sunset.
- **Best for families**: Vathí (calm bay) or Kamares (port access, space).
- **No car?** Stay central in Apollonia. The bus reaches everywhere.

Most of the conversations we have with first-time visitors come back to this question, and the answer is usually the same: Apollonia. Here is why, and where the other options earn their place.

## Apollonia — central, social, walkable

The capital village, in the middle of the island. The pedestrian alley runs through it, end to end. Most of the better restaurants are here. The KTEL buses to every beach leave from here. You can walk to dinner. You sleep where the light is gentlest, on a hill, away from the harbour glare.

**Best for**: first-time visitors, couples, foodies, travellers without a car, repeat visitors who already love it.
**Trade-off**: none, really, if you choose your block carefully — some places sit on the bar lane and stay loud. Streets one alley over are silent by midnight.

## Artemónas — quieter than Apollonia, ten minutes' walk away

Twenty minutes uphill from Apollonia, connected by the marble pedestrian alley. Slightly more genteel — bigger mansions, more bougainvillea, fewer bars. You still have the bus connections via Apollonia, you still have several great restaurants, you just have a calmer evening.

**Best for**: couples who want privacy without isolation, returning visitors, slow-travel weeks.
**Trade-off**: slightly less going-out energy. For some, that's the feature.

## Kamares — beach + harbour

The port. Long sandy beach and a row of harbour tavernas. Easy ferry day, easy beach mornings. The downside: everything else takes a drive or a bus.

**Best for**: very short stays (one or two nights), or travellers whose main interest is the beach itself.
**Trade-off**: every village dinner becomes a logistics decision.

## Platis Gialós or Vathí — beach-locked

The two main south-coast beach hamlets. You wake up on the sand. You also need a car for almost everything else. Platis Gialós is the larger of the two, with more on-the-beach options. Vathí is calmer, prettier, more family-friendly.

**Best for**: families with very young children, beach-first travellers, anyone whose week revolves around swimming.
**Trade-off**: the rhythm of the island happens elsewhere — the villages, the food, the alleys.

## Kastro — the medieval cliff village

A handful of properties exist inside Kastro. It is breathtaking. It is also remote: one taverna inside the village, no parking, and a 25-minute drive back to Apollonia in the dark.

**Best for**: writers, photographers, second-trip travellers who already know the island.
**Trade-off**: logistics get serious. Beautiful by day, atmospheric by sunset, demanding after dark.

## What to consider when you choose

- **How long is your trip?** Three to five nights → Apollonia. Seven plus → Apollonia or Artemónas. One or two nights as a stopover → Kamares.
- **Are you driving?** No car → Apollonia or Kamares. Car → anywhere.
- **What time do you want dinner?** Walk-to-dinner ease → Apollonia or Artemónas. Drive-to-dinner okay → anywhere.
- **What's your priority?** Atmosphere and food → Apollonia. Beach mornings → Platis Gialós or Vathí. Quiet → Artemónas. Drama → Kastro.

## The honest summary

For most travellers, Apollonia is the right answer. It puts you within a five-minute walk of dinner, fifteen minutes' drive of every beach, and ten minutes' walk of every other village. It is the village that holds the rhythm of the island.
