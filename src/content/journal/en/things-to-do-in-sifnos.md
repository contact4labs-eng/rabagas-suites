The phrase "things to do" is awkward on an island like Sifnos. You don't really *do* the island; you slow down enough to let it happen. That said, there are specific things worth your time. Here are eleven.

## 1. Walk the alley from Apollonia to Artemónas at golden hour

Twenty minutes, slightly uphill, perfectly paved with marble flagstones. It starts at the central platía in Apollonia, passes the cafés and the small shops, ends in Artemónas where the lanes get narrower and the houses get prettier. Best done around 19:30 in summer, when the light comes in low over the whitewash.

## 2. Eat chickpeas (revithada) on a Sunday

Revithada is the dish Sifnos is most known for: chickpeas baked overnight in a sealed clay pot in a wood oven. Traditionally Sunday food. Every taverna does its own version. Order it once, then once more.

## 3. Swim at Vathí before noon, again at sunset

Vathí is a wide, shallow bay on the south coast — long curve of sand, water you can wade out twenty metres in. The light is best either side of the heat. There are two or three good tavernas on the sand.

## 4. Walk to Chrysopigí from the next bay over

The whitewashed monastery on a tied island is the photograph everyone takes. Don't drive to the parking lot. Park at Apokoftó or Faros and walk along the coast for ten minutes. It changes how you see it.

## 5. Go to Kastro at sunset, not at noon

Kastro is the medieval village on the east coast — a single fortified line of houses sitting on a clifftop. Walk through, stay for the sunset, drink something at the small bar that looks down to the sea. Skip the daytime visit.

## 6. Try one pottery workshop

Sifnian ceramics have a continuous history that runs back to antiquity. Workshops in Vathí and Artemónas are open in the afternoons; the courteous thing is to buy something small.

## 7. Walk one of the marked paths

Sifnos has a network of well-marked walking trails between villages. Path 3 (Apollonia → Kastro) is the gentlest and the most rewarding for first walkers. Two hours, flat to rolling, you end up at the sea.

## 8. Have one dinner away from a beach

Apollonia and Artemónas have the island's best food. Beach tavernas are pleasant; the village kitchens are where things get serious. Ask anyone who lives here for their three favourite and pick the one you can walk to.

## 9. Take a small boat to a beach you can't drive to

Several local skippers run half-day private boats from Kamares or Faros. The west coast has small inlets — Fykiada is one — that are only reached by sea. We can put you in touch with people we trust.

## 10. Buy honey, capers and a bottle of *amýgdalo*

The local almond drink (*amýgdalo*) is unusual and very Sifnian — you'll see bottles of it in every general store. Honey from the upper valleys and salt-cured capers from rock walls are the other two things worth taking home.

## 11. Do one whole afternoon of nothing

The most underrated thing on the island. Read on a terrace. Watch the cats. Eat figs. The Aegean is good at giving back to people who slow down enough to receive it.

## What to skip

- Cars to Kastro — you cannot drive in; park outside.
- Day-tripping Milos — possible, but you'll spend the day on ferries.
- Booking every taverna before arriving — leave a couple of nights open.
