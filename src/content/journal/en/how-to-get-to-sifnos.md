You picked Sifnos. Good. Now you are trying to figure out how to get there. The internet will give you ten contradictory answers in five minutes. Here is the short version.

## You go by ferry, from Piraeus

There is no airport on Sifnos and there will not be one. The only regular way to reach the island is by sea, from the port of Piraeus in Athens. That is the rule. Everything else is a detail.

Piraeus is connected to the centre of Athens by metro Line 1 (about 25 minutes from Monastiraki) and is a short taxi from Athens International Airport (about 45 minutes outside rush hour). If you are flying in the same day, give yourself at least three hours of buffer between your landing and the ferry departure.

## High-speed or conventional?

Two kinds of ferry serve Sifnos.

- **Conventional ferries** (Bluestar Ferries) take roughly four to five hours from Piraeus, run year-round, and cost the least. They are larger, more stable in weather, and pleasant if you treat the crossing as part of the holiday.
- **High-speed ferries** (Seajets, Cyclades Fast) take roughly two and a half to three hours and cost more. They are slightly more sensitive to wind, so summer cancellations or rerouting happen occasionally.

Either is fine. If it is your first time, the conventional ferry is the more relaxed choice. Book a few weeks ahead in July and August.

## You arrive in Kamares — Apollonia is ten minutes uphill

The ferry docks at Kamares, the harbour on the west coast of the island. Kamares is not where most visitors stay. The island's central village, Apollonia, is a ten-minute drive up the hill and is the place you want to be.

There is a public KTEL bus that meets every ferry. Buses run frequently in summer and connect Kamares to Apollonia and most of the major beaches. Taxis are limited — there are only a handful of them on the island, and they are usually pre-booked.

## What about the helicopter?

A private helicopter service does land on Sifnos. It is operated as a charter, not as a scheduled flight. It is occasionally the right choice for guests with very short windows, and it is weather-dependent. We can put you in touch.

## Quick answers

- **Is there an airport?** No, and there are no plans for one.
- **How long is the ferry?** Roughly 2.5–3 hours by high-speed, 4–5 hours conventional.
- **Do ferries run in winter?** Yes — less frequently (typically once a day), and sea conditions can disrupt schedules from November through March.
- **Do I need a car on Sifnos?** Only if your base is far from a village. From Apollonia, the bus and a couple of taxi rides cover most beach days.
