You picked Sifnos. Good. Now you are trying to figure out how to get there, and the internet is not helping — one site gives one travel time, another gives a completely different one. Here is the short version, in plain terms.

## You go by ferry, from Piraeus

There is no airport on Sifnos and there will not be one. The only regular way to reach the island is by sea, from the port of Piraeus in Athens. Ferries run year-round, with more frequent crossings in summer. If you are already island-hopping in the Cyclades, you can also reach Sifnos by ferry from Milos, Paros, Serifos, or Mykonos.

Piraeus is connected to central Athens by metro Line 1 (about 25 minutes from Monastiraki) and is a short taxi from Athens International Airport (about 45 minutes outside rush hour). If you are flying in the same day, give yourself at least three hours of buffer between your landing and the ferry departure.

## High-speed or conventional?

Two kinds of ferry serve Sifnos.

**Conventional ferries** (Blue Star Ferries) take roughly four to five hours from Piraeus, run year-round, and cost the least. They are larger, more stable in weather, and pleasant if you treat the crossing as part of the holiday — bring a book and watch the islands go by.

**High-speed ferries** (Seajets, Cyclades Fast) take roughly two and a half to three hours and cost more. They are slightly more sensitive to wind, so summer cancellations or rerouting happen occasionally.

Either is fine. If it is your first time, the conventional ferry is the more relaxed choice. Book a few weeks ahead in July and August.

## You arrive in Kamares — Apollonia is ten minutes uphill

The ferry docks at Kamares, the harbour on the west coast. Kamares is not where most visitors stay; it's a small port with a sandy beach and a row of harbour tavernas, perfect for a first meal but not the place to base a week.

The island's central village, Apollonia, is a ten-minute drive up the hill and is the place you want to be. There is a public KTEL bus that meets every ferry. Buses run frequently in summer and connect Kamares to Apollonia and most of the main beaches (Platis Gialós, Vathí, Faros, Chrysopigí). Taxis are limited — there are only a handful on the island, and they are usually pre-booked.

## What about the helicopter?

A private helicopter service does land on Sifnos. It is operated as a charter, not as a scheduled flight, and it is weather-dependent. It is occasionally the right choice for guests with very short windows. We can put you in touch.

## Quick answers

**Is there an airport?** No, and there are no plans for one.

**How long is the ferry?** Roughly 2.5–3 hours by high-speed, 4–5 hours by conventional.

**Do ferries run in winter?** Yes — less frequently (typically once a day), and sea conditions can disrupt schedules from November through March.

**What is the cheapest way to reach Sifnos?** Conventional ferry from Piraeus. The cabin upgrades are nice but the deck seats are perfectly fine on a calm day.

**Do I need a car on Sifnos?** Only if your base is far from a village. From Apollonia, the bus and a couple of taxi rides cover most beach days. A scooter or small car helps for half-day beach-hopping.

**Can I island-hop from Sifnos?** Yes. Day trips to Milos and Kimolos are common; longer hops to Paros, Naxos, or Serifos work if you build them into your dates.
